QUESTION: What are the conditions under which a buyer has buyer protection?
ANSWER: A buyer has buyer protection under certain conditions which allows them to have disputes handled, and to have the amount they have paid in a transaction refunded.

QUESTION: What are the voluntary repayments that a seller can make to a buyer?
ANSWER: If a transaction needs to be fully or partly repaid by the Seller due to an agreement between Seller and Buyer (e.g., the Buyer has been granted a right of return by the Seller), it is up to the Seller to pay back part or full amount to the Buyer up to 1000 SEK. The Seller must make refunds only through Tradera's payment solution, and not through other payment methods.
URL: https://info.tradera.com/en/security-center/betalningsvillkor-eng/

QUESTION: Is there a limit to the extent of refunds that a seller can make?
ANSWER: Yes, the extent of refunds is limited, and the Seller may not refund transactions exceeding 1000 SEK. If the Seller needs to refund transactions in excess of 1000 SEK, they must first contact Tradera's customer service to receive instructions.

QUESTION: What is Tradera's prevailing Seller Protection?
ANSWER: Tradera's prevailing Seller Protection is a policy that protects sellers by offering them reimbursement for certain losses they encounter such as unauthorized charges, credit card fraud, and other chargebacks.

QUESTION: Under what circumstances will transactions not be eligible for Seller Protection?
