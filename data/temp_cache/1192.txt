QUESTION: What happens if a dispute settlement procedure is terminated in favor of the Buyer?
ANSWER: If a dispute settlement procedure is terminated in favor of the Buyer, the Buyer will be entitled to a refund, either in full or in part, of the amount paid by the Buyer for an item. Tradera will refund the amount to the Buyer and reserves the right to invoice the Seller for the amount of refund owed.

QUESTION: What happens if a dispute settlement procedure is terminated in favor of the Seller?
ANSWER: If a dispute settlement procedure is terminated in favor of the Seller, the Buyer will not be entitled to a refund through Tradera's dispute settlement procedure. However, the Buyer and Seller are free to find another solution to a dispute in another forum.

QUESTION: What is Tradera's seller protection and what products are covered under it?
ANSWER: Tradera's seller protection is a program that makes it possible for a Seller to keep the payment received under certain conditions, even if the Buyer claims that they have never received an item or that the item is not in accordance with the Seller's description. However, the following products are not covered under Tradera's seller protection: sale of properties, sale of vehicles except for private portable light vehicles used for recreational purposes, sale of customized objects, and sale of items equivalent to cash like gift cards.

QUESTION: What is the purpose of Clause 6(d) in Tradera's buyer protection policy?
ANSWER: Clause 6(d) in Tradera's buyer protection policy describes products that are not covered by Tradera's buyer protection.
