QUESTION: What is the dispute settlement system on Tradera's platform?
ANSWER: Tradera has a dispute settlement system in case there's a problem between a buyer and a seller. The buyer and seller communicate with each other through the system to try to find a solution. If they don't come to a resolution within ten days, the buyer may escalate the dispute to Tradera.

QUESTION: What happens if the buyer doesn't escalate the dispute to Tradera?
ANSWER: If the buyer doesn't escalate the dispute to Tradera within ten days, the dispute settlement procedure will automatically be terminated in the seller's favor.

QUESTION: How does Tradera handle escalated disputes?
ANSWER: If a dispute is escalated to Tradera, Tradera will contact both the buyer and seller to understand what's happened. They may ask both parties for supporting evidence, like additional documentations concerning the goods or transactions, before making a decision.

QUESTION: What is the responsibility of the buyer in a dispute settlement procedure?
ANSWER: The buyer is responsible for providing evidence to show that there are errors in the service provided by the seller, or the item sold by the seller. They need to prove that the goods differ from the seller's description.
